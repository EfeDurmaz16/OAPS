# OAPS Royalty-Free Patent Pledge

## Draft Intent

OAPS is intended to be developed as an open standard with royalty-free implementation rights for required claims contributed by participating authors and maintainers.

This document is a draft policy statement, not final legal language.

## Guiding Principle

Anyone implementing the required parts of the OAPS specification should be able to do so without needing a patent license fee from contributors who helped define those required parts.

## Draft Commitment

Contributors to the OAPS specification are expected to support a royalty-free, non-discriminatory implementation posture for any patent claims they necessarily contribute to the required portions of the specification.

## Scope

This pledge is intended to apply to:

- core semantics
- required binding behavior
- required profile behavior
- required conformance behavior

It is not intended to force open licensing of unrelated implementation details, optional extensions, trade secrets, or separate products.

## Why This Matters

OAPS will not be perceived as a real open standard unless implementers believe they can build against it safely.

This matters for:

- outside contributors
- cosigner companies
- independent implementers
- standards bodies or future neutral foundation structures

## Next Step

Before broad external cosigner outreach, this draft intent should be converted into finalized policy text reviewed with counsel so that OAPS can present a credible royalty-free standardization posture.
